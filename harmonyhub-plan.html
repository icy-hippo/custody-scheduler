Week-by-Week Timeline

Week 1 — June 14–20 · Mobile Foundation

Goal: App runs correctly on a real Android phone

  • Configure Capacitor (app ID, name, icon, splash screen)
  • Build APK and install on a real Android device
  • Fix mobile responsiveness — buttons, font sizes, layout on small screens
  • Fix hover states that don't work on touch screens
  • Test every feature end-to-end on the phone

Week 2 — June 21–27 · Push Notifications + Auth Polish

Goal: App feels native, not like a website

  • Integrate @capacitor/push-notifications (replace browser bell)
  • Add handoff reminders — notify 24hr before custody switchover
  • Implement "stay logged in" / persistent auth on mobile
  • Fix any Firebase auth quirks in Android WebView
  • Add loading states and skeleton screens

Week 3 — June 28 – July 4 · Feature Completion

Goal: App is feature-complete for v1.0 launch

  • Child "what's changing" alert when parent edits upcoming event
  • Onboarding flow — new users understand the app in 60 seconds
  • Empty states — meaningful UI before any data exists
  • Basic error handling (lost connection, failed saves)
  • Communication log export (download message history as PDF/text)

Week 4 — July 5–11 · Beta Testing

Goal: Real people use it and report bugs

  • Recruit 5–10 families (friends, family, PISD contacts, autism support groups)
  • Distribute APK via Google Drive or Firebase App Distribution
  • Create a simple feedback form (Google Form)
  • Add "Report a Bug" button inside the app
  • Fix top 5 issues reported by testers
  • Performance audit — load time on slower/older Android phones

Week 5 — July 12–18 · Play Store Submission

Goal: App submitted — this week has a hard deadline

  • Write Privacy Policy (required — use privacypolicygenerator.info)
  • Write Terms of Service
  • Create Play Store listing: screenshots, description, content rating
  • Generate signed AAB (Android App Bundle) for Play Store
  • Create Google Play Developer account ($25 one-time fee)
  • Submit to Play Store by July 14 at the latest

Week 6 — July 19–25 · Review Buffer

Goal: Pass Play Store review (first reviews take 7–14 days)

  • Respond to any rejection feedback from Play Store reviewers
  • Fix flagged issues and resubmit if needed
  • Continue beta user feedback loop
  • Prepare launch communications (who you'll notify on launch day)

Week 7 — July 26–31 · Launch

Goal: Live on Play Store with real users

  • App approved and published on Play Store
  • Share with beta testers and initial user group
  • Monitor Firebase console for errors and crashes
  • Gather first-week user feedback
  • Document v2.0 roadmap (iOS, more features)

Risk Register

Risk Likelihood Impact Mitigation
Play Store review takes longer than 14 days Medium High Submit by July 12, not July 18. Build buffer into the plan.
Mobile UI needs major rework Medium Medium Test on real device in Week 1, not Week 3.
Onboarding too confusing for new users High High Beta test with someone who has never seen the app. Watch them use it without explaining anything.
Play Store rejects app for policy violation Low High Follow content policy carefully, include privacy policy, don't collect more data than needed.
Firebase costs at scale Low Low Free tier handles ~50k reads/day. Fine for launch. Set billing alerts.
Push notifications complex on Android Medium Medium Capacitor handles most of it. FCM + VAPID key already partially configured.
